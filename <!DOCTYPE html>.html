The Darian calendar is a proposed system of timekeeping designed to serve the needs of any possible future human settlers on the planet Mars.

It was created by aerospace engineer, political scientist, and space jurist Thomas Gangale in 1985 and named by him after his son Darius.

It was first published in June 1986. In 1998 at the founding convention of the Mars Society the calendar was presented as one of two calendar options to be considered along with eighteen other factors to consider for the colonization of Mars.

Darian Calendar Layout:
  • Months 24 Total
  • Days are called "Sols"
  • The calendar maintains a seven-sol week.
  • The Martian year begins near the equinox marking spring in the northern hemisphere of the planet
